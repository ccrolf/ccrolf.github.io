There are different types of sources of hard work. This post is focused on the most common one: lack of focus. Sometimes, deadlines, promotions, or time-zone differences make overtime a necessity. But constant hard work in the tech industry is often a sign of a lack of focus. 

How well do you know your manager's, skip-manager's, and senior IC's priorities? How do you know what to focus on during the day/week/month if you don't know then and align to it?

Individual success often relies on hard work, but organizations that require regular overtime to meet deadlines are unsustainable. They need a constant supply of fresh people to put through the grinder.

There's nothing admirable about putting in long hours. It just means you get ahead of people with families, hobbies, a life.

If a leader can't do their job in X hours per week. The people they lead certainly can't do it.
