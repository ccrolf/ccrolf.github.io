If you want your team's AI adoption fail, here's a simple recipe: focus on token use, obsess over model versions, and ignore the fundamentals of developer productivity.

# Thought experiment 
Imagine you're part of a team of ten software developers. One day your manager tells you that ten more developers are joining next week. What will happen to reliability and productivity?

My guess: both crater within weeks. A few months later, half of your old team mates have left. Burned out by spending their days reviewing pull-requests and their nights resolving incidents.


What if your manager had instead asked "We got ten open headcount. But how can we double the team without sacrificing productivity and reliability?"

Most engineers I know would be overjoyed at that question. Reducing incident risk and paying down tech debt is exactly the type of work that gets left on the backburner until it can no longer be ignored.


# Developer productivity is hard
No team can double productivity over night, regardless of if they use people or AI. Every team would need to invest in architecture improvements, test quality, CI/CD reliability, early incident detection, and faster incident resolution. This is exactly the kind of work that's worthy of the term 'software engineering'.

Done right, AI adoption is a win/win for engineering and management. I've led developer productivity, including AI adoption, across 50 engineers for the past two years. We've increased developer productivity by 100% and developer satisfaction by 50%, so far.

But if you still want your team's AI adoption to fail, here's an even simpler recipe: stop asking, stop listening, and start mandating.
