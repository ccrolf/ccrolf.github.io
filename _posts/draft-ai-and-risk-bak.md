Have you also felt the huge cognitive dissonance between the productivity promises of AI and the actual results? If there's so much productivity, where are the products?, where is the business impact?, where are all the $1M MRR single-person startups?

At the same time, we are creating hobby projects like never before. So AI has clearly demonstrated great potential, so what is going on?


# Productivity vs Risk

Dynamically typed languages and garbage collection have had a massive impact on productivity across the industry. Java enabled larger teams and more engineers, arguably fuelling the internet boom. Javascript and Python enabled much delivery of MVPs, allowing startups to move faster.

However, the benefits of these technologies look very different when mapping them against the main difference between established companies and startups: risk tolerance.

Dynamic typing and garbage collection exhibit almost opposite benefits. The higher the risk tolerance, the higher the productivity benefit of dynamic typing. For garbage collection, the benefit is greatest when the risk tolerance is lowest.

Can this model of looking at productivity explain why AI is incredibly for prototyping and hobby projects, but not particularly impactful for products?

![Productivity gain vs Risk tolerance chart]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/risk-productivity-ai.png)

We can further extend the chart to cover extreme high risk tolerance, like that of hobby projects. This illustrates why enterprises struggle to get a lot of value from AI, and startups see huge benefits.

# What this means
Does this mean AI is doomed to not be particularly impactful at large companies? No, it means more work needs to shift from being low risk to high risk. This cannot be done without changing the overall workflow from ideation to production.

The fundamental shift required is to consider productivity of the entire software development lifecycle from idea to polished product. Currently, this involves multiple iterations. Using AI for rapid prototyping, ideally with customers in the room, a polished design that solves the problem can be developed in a single iteration instead of requiring multiple iterations on the feature designs.
